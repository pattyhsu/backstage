Condition Tiers — T1 → T6

Worst → best. T5 splits into sub-tiers by how many of kitchen / bath are updated.

T1Distressed
Heavily deteriorated or barely habitable. Deferred maintenance throughout, failing systems, original everything. Sells at the bottom of the market.
T2Dated (livable)
Fully livable but visibly old. Original kitchen, baths, and flooring show their age — needs cosmetic updating, not structural work.
T3Modern (owner-grade)
Modern to an owner's everyday taste — builder / big-box finishes (mid-tier counters, stock cabinets, laminate or basic tile). Current and move-in fine, but not staged-to-sell designer quality.
T4Lived-in flip-quality
Designer / flip-grade finishes (quartz, custom cabinetry, cohesive styling) — a step above T3 — but showing lived-in wear. A prior flip that's since been occupied, or an owner who renovated to flip standards.
T5Half-flip — partial reno
Partially renovated. Sub-tier records how much of the value-driving rooms (kitchen + bath) are done:
T5-AKitchen and bath both updated — nicest of the half-flips.
T5-BOne of kitchen / bath updated — the true half-flip.
T5-CBoth kitchen and bath original — flip opportunity.
T6Full flip
Renovated top-to-bottom with designer / flip-quality finishes. Move-in turnkey; commands the top of the market.
⊘Uncategorized
Can't be fairly graded — exterior-only or too few interior photos. Click the active tier again (or the ⊘ Uncat button) to clear it; these land under the Uncategorized filter rather than being forced into a tier.
